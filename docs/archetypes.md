# Voter Archetypes

> **Source:** Neto, Felipe. *Brasil no Espelho.* Quaest, 2024. Chapter 8: "O Brasil em segmentos."
> Cluster analysis on ~10,000 respondents using 197 variables. The segmentation is identity-based — defined by values, attitudes, and voting behavior, not demographics. All percentages cited in this document are from Chapter 8 unless otherwise noted.
>
> **Note on editability:** If any segment description feels politically inaccurate or uncharitable, edit it directly on GitHub. These descriptions will be used to construct voter prompts — they need to be realistic, not caricatures.

The nine segments were generated by cluster analysis on ~10,000 survey respondents using 197 variables covering values, attitudes, religion, economics, and political behavior. The segmentation is **identity-based, not demographic** — two people with identical demographics can be in different segments based on what drives their vote.

The chart below shows segment size (Y axis) and share that voted for Lula in the 2022 second round (X axis).

## Individual profiles

### 1. Conservador cristão — 27%

**The largest segment.** Mostly evangelical, plus conservative Catholics who attend church weekly.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 27% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 40% |
| Region | Periphery of large cities, interior |
| Religion | 56% attend church weekly (highest of all segments) |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 44 years, trabalhador(a) do comércio, city of medium size, 3 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- Order, obedience, respect for elders
- Traditional family: 77% agree "man is the provider" (highest of all)
- 78% oppose marijuana legalization
- Strong concern with public security

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"minha prioridade é segurança pública e defesa dos valores da família"*
- Level 4 full profile includes: evangelical identity, weekly church attendance, opposition to abortion and "gender ideology" in schools

---

### 2. Classes D e E — 23%

**The second largest segment.** State-dependent low-income voters, concentrated in North and Northeast.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 23% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 75% |
| Region | North, Northeast, periphery of Southeast cities |
| Income | Significantly below average |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 42 years, trabalhador(a) informal, periphery, 1 minimum wage |

**Defining values (from book):**
- 37% receive Bolsa Família (vs. 22% national average)
- 56% support taxing all to help the poor
- Paradox: 73% believe government programs make people less willing to work
- Most sensitive to food price inflation and unemployment

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"minha prioridade é saúde pública e programas sociais"*
- Level 4 includes: Bolsa Família dependency, 1 minimum wage income, desire for state that provides

---

### 3. Agro — 13%

**Third largest.** Rural identity — from large producers to anyone who identifies with sertanejo culture.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 13% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 25% |
| Region | Center-West, South, interior of SP and MG |
| Income | Varied — large producers to rural workers |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 52 years, produtor(a) rural, zona rural, 7 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- 71% self-identify as traditional/conservative (second only to extrema direita)
- 60% oppose facilitating gun access (more permissive than national 70%)
- Most powerful congressional bloc — can block environmental legislation
- Strong Bolsonaro supporters

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"me preocupo com as regulações que travam o agronegócio"*
- Level 4 includes: opposition to new indigenous land demarcations, defense of rural producer

---

### 4. Progressista — 11%

**Most distinct segment from the rest of Brazil.** Defined by opposition to conservative norms.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 11% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 75% |
| Gender | **Book states: more women** |
| Age | **Book states: younger** |
| Region | Southeast capitals |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | feminino, 27 years, designer gráfica, capital, 4 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- Only 3% agree a woman who aborts should be imprisoned (vs. 52% national)
- 65% disagree Brazil is a racial democracy
- 69% agree "faith worth more than science" — lowest of all (vs. 86% national)
- Most concerned with climate crisis and minority rights
- Less TV, more digital

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"minha maior preocupação é com mudanças climáticas e direitos das minorias"*
- Level 4 includes: LGBTQ+ support, climate urgency, racial quotas, low religiosity

---

### 5. Militante de esquerda — 7%

**Small but highly mobilized.** Strongest party identification of all segments (PT).

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 7% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 90% |
| Education | Above average |
| Region | Southeast, Northeast |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 38 years, professor(a) universitário(a), capital, 6 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- Mobilized by 2016 Dilma impeachment — see it as institutional coup
- "Us vs. them" worldview: government actions always positive, opposition always negative
- 63% say country grew during survey period (vs. 52% national)
- 82% satisfied with their life (highest, tied with empresários)

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"me preocupo muito com desigualdade social e direitos trabalhistas"*
- Level 4 includes: PT identification, impeachment framing, opposition to privatization

---

### 6. Empresário — 6%

**Economic elite minority.** Mostly white, older, South and Southeast.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 6% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 20% |
| Income | High |
| Region | South, Southeast |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 55 years, empresário(a), capital, 20 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- 69% pro-privatization (second only to liberal social at 70%)
- Against government benefits to the poor
- Sees itself as modern (digital info consumption)
- Paradox: 92% say God is very important (despite being among least religious)

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"minha prioridade é responsabilidade fiscal e ambiente de negócios"*
- Level 4 includes: private sector generates jobs, fiscal responsibility, privatization

---

### 7. Liberal social — 5%

**The "third way" segment.** Anti-polarization, voted Tebet 2022 first round, Lula second round.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 5% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 65% (voted Lula primarily to stop Bolsonaro) |
| Income | Above average |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 45 years, profissional liberal, capital, 10 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- 89% support privatizing public health and education (41pp above national average — most distinctive stat)
- 65% vote only because mandatory — highest opposition to compulsory voting
- 85% support death penalty for rape — surprisingly punitive for a "liberal" segment
- Wants end to polarization, Brazil's international reputation

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"quero candidatos comprometidos com democracia e fim da polarização"*
- Level 4 includes: voted Tebet/Lula, pro-market but pro-democracy, rejects PT-PL binary

---

### 8. Empreendedor individual — 5%

**New segment, unique to this research.** Gig economy workers who see themselves as entrepreneurs.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 5% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | 50% |
| Gender | **Book states: mostly men** |
| Region | South, Southeast, Norte/Nordeste |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | masculino, 33 years, motorista de aplicativo, city of medium size, 3 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- No employment contract, no benefits — sees this as entrepreneurship not precarity
- 72% support wage differences to incentivize effort
- 71% "help only those who deserve it"
- 63% pro-privatization
- Resistant to unions, trusts influencers over institutions
- Negative view of politics and Brazilian democracy

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"me preocupo muito com a carga tributária e a burocracia para quem é MEI"*
- Level 4 includes: "corri atrás, ninguém me deu nada", anti-bureaucracy, anti-tax

---

### 9. Extrema direita — 3%

**Smallest but most distinctive segment.** The only segment that does not defend democracy.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Size | 3% of electorate |
| Lula 2022 | ~5% |
| Fixed profile (Exp 1) | 48 years, trabalhador(a) autônomo(a), city of medium size, 4 minimum wages |

**Defining values (from book):**
- 100% agree dictatorship can be preferable to democracy — the only segment with this unanimity
- 100% say God is very important (even higher than conservadores cristãos at 99%)
- 83% oppose marijuana legalization (highest of all)
- Prefer strong central power, nationalist, "impose order"
- **Important methodological note**: the book explicitly distinguishes this segment from regular conservatives and Bolsonaro voters. Calling all Bolsonaro voters "extrema direita" is technically wrong — the majority are conservadores cristãos and agro, who do support democracy.

**How they appear in queries:**
- Level 2 issue: *"quero candidatos que defendam ordem, segurança e os valores cristãos"*
- Level 4 includes: Bolsonaro persecution narrative, distrust of STF and media, anti-institutional
